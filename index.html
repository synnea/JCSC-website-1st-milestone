About

The Japanese Culture and Social Club Berlin was first founded in 2000 by a group of five students majoring in Japanese studies at the University of Humboldt. The initial goal of the club was to meet up once a month to discuss Japanese culture news, study the language, and watch Japanese films.

In 2005, with the backing of our first major sponsor, AnimA Studios, we organized our first public event with the screening of the Ghibli classic anime movie "Spirited Away."

Since then, we have accumulated over 10 different sponsors and organize two or three events every month, ranging from tea ceremony to samurai sword practice.

Our goal and vision is to bring diverse events to Berlin and make them easily accessible to everyone who is interested. Japan is home to thousands of years of refined art, architecture, literature, poetry and design - and there's something for everyone!

Membership is and will remain free - with membership at our club, you automatically sign up to our email list and get an automatic 10% discount on all club events!
